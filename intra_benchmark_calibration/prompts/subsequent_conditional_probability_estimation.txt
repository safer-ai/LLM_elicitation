<refinement_task>
Please review your previous estimate in light of the other experts' perspectives:

**Consider the following:**

1. **Points of Agreement:** Where do the expert estimates cluster? What reasoning do multiple experts share?

2. **Points of Disagreement:** Where do estimates diverge significantly? What explains these differences in perspective?

3. **New Insights:** Did other experts identify factors you overlooked? Did they interpret the difficulty gap differently?

4. **Reassess Your Position:**
   - Should you adjust your estimate based on compelling arguments from others?
   - Or do you maintain your position because you have strong justification?
   - Be willing to change your mind if presented with better reasoning
   - But also be willing to hold your ground if you have solid justification

5. **Convergence Check:**
   - Are we converging toward a consensus? If so, what probability range?
   - If not, what core disagreements remain about task difficulty or capability transfer?

**You may:**
- Increase your estimate if others convinced you of stronger capability transfer
- Decrease your estimate if others identified capability gaps you missed
- Maintain your estimate if you have strong justification and disagree with others' reasoning
- Narrow or widen your uncertainty bounds based on the discussion
</refinement_task>

<reasoning_structure>
Please follow this structured refinement process:

**Step 1: Synthesis of Group Reasoning**
- What are the main arguments and evidence from other experts?
- Which arguments are most compelling? Which are less convincing?
- What is the emerging consensus (if any)?

**Step 2: Critical Comparison to Your Previous Estimate**
- How does your previous estimate compare to the group?
- If you're an outlier, what explains the difference? Are you considering something others missed, or did you overlook something?
- If you're in the mainstream, do the arguments strengthen or weaken your confidence?

**Step 3: Updated Probability Assessment**
- Based on the full discussion, what is your revised probability estimate?
- How has your uncertainty changed? (narrower or wider bounds?)
- What key factors drive your final estimate?

**Step 4: Justification**
- Explain your reasoning: Why this estimate?
- Address key disagreements: Why do you agree or disagree with outlier positions?
- What would it take to change your estimate further?
</reasoning_structure>

<distribution_fitting_note>
**Important**: Your estimates will be used to fit a Beta distribution with support [0, 1] to characterise your subjective probability distribution. We elicit three percentiles:
- **25th percentile**: The lower quartile - 25% of the probability mass lies below this value
- **50th percentile**: The median - the midpoint of your distribution
- **75th percentile**: The upper quartile - 75% of the probability mass lies below this value

Together, the 25th and 75th percentiles encompass the inner 50% of your probability mass, providing a meaningful uncertainty interval around your median estimate.
</distribution_fitting_note>
