<reasoning_structure>
Please follow this structured reasoning process:

**Phase 1: Establish Ransom Range**

Consider the factors that determine ransom amounts:
- What is the baseline mean ransom without LLM assistance, and why?
- What factors currently influence ransom amounts? (Consider: target valuation, leverage/threat credibility, attacker sophistication, victim assessment)
- At which steps in the attack chain could LLM assistance increase the ransom amount?

Establish bounds:
- What is the LOWEST reasonable mean ransom? (LLM provides minimal uplift, similar to baseline)
- What is the HIGHEST reasonable mean ransom? (LLM enables significantly better targeting, valuation, and leverage)

**Phase 2: Confidence Check**

Critically examine your reasoning:
- Am I overestimating how much LLM assistance would increase ransom amounts?
- Am I underestimating the remaining constraints (victim payment capacity, insurance limits, law enforcement pressure)?
- What does the ratio of my estimate to baseline imply? Is that multiplier justified?

**Phase 3: Reality Check**

Test against practical expectations:
- Does my estimate align with historical data on ransom payments for this type of attack?
- What would it mean for ransom amounts to change by this factor? Is that plausible?
- Are there analogous cases where new tools dramatically changed ransom demands?
</reasoning_structure>
