<reasoning_structure>
Please follow this structured reasoning process:

**Phase 1: Establish Damage Range**

Consider the factors that determine attack impact:
- What is the baseline mean damage without LLM assistance, and why?
- What factors currently limit the damage from successful attacks? (Consider: depth of access, scope of compromise, speed of detection, attacker efficiency)
- At which steps in the attack chain could LLM assistance increase impact?

Establish bounds:
- What is the LOWEST reasonable mean damage? (LLM provides minimal impact uplift, similar to baseline)
- What is the HIGHEST reasonable mean damage? (LLM enables significantly deeper/broader compromise)

**Phase 2: Confidence Check**

Critically examine your reasoning:
- Am I overestimating how much LLM assistance would increase attack impact?
- Am I underestimating the remaining constraints (detection, incident response, target hardening)?
- What does the ratio of my estimate to baseline imply? Is that multiplier justified?

**Phase 3: Reality Check**

Test against practical expectations:
- Does my estimate align with historical data on economic impact for this type of attack?
- What would it mean for attack damage to change by this factor? Is that plausible?
- Are there analogous cases where new tools dramatically changed attack impact?
</reasoning_structure>
