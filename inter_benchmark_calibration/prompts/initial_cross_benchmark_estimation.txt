<probability_estimation_task>
Based on this analysis, please answer the following:

**Your task is to estimate a CONDITIONAL PROBABILITY:**

P(model can solve the target task | model demonstrates the source capabilities shown above)

This is NOT asking about the absolute difficulty of the target task. This is asking: given what we KNOW about the model's capabilities from its source benchmark performance, what is the probability it can solve this specific target task on a different benchmark?

Key considerations:
- The source and target benchmarks test DIFFERENT domains/skills, so capability transfer is not guaranteed
- However, there are often latent general capabilities (reasoning, code understanding, problem decomposition) that correlate across benchmarks
- A model scoring highly on a challenging source benchmark likely has strong general capabilities
- The question is whether those general capabilities are sufficient for this specific target task
- Consider both the model's capability level AND the specific demands of the target task
</probability_estimation_task>

<reasoning_structure>
Please follow this structured reasoning process:

**Phase 1: Establish Probability Ranges**

First, consider the extreme cases:
- What is the LOWEST reasonable probability? Consider scenarios where source capabilities don't transfer.
- What is the HIGHEST reasonable probability? Consider scenarios where general capabilities strongly predict target performance.
- What range does this suggest?

Factors to weigh:
- Domain similarity between source and target benchmarks
- The model's relative standing on the source benchmark
- Difficulty level of the target task within its benchmark
- Strength of latent capability correlations across these specific domains

**Phase 2: Refine and Check for Over/Under-confidence**

Critically examine your reasoning:
- Am I assuming too much cross-domain transfer? Different benchmarks can test genuinely different capabilities.
- Am I assuming too little transfer? Models with strong source performance often have broad general capabilities.
- What does empirical evidence suggest about correlations between these types of benchmarks?

**Phase 3: Reality Check**

Test against practical expectations:
- If I say 80%: This means 4 out of 5 models at this source level would solve this target task. Is that plausible?
- If I say 50%: This means it's a coin flip whether source capabilities predict target success. Does that match the domain gap?
- If I say 20%: This means the source performance is a weak predictor for this specific target task. Is the gap really that large?
</reasoning_structure>
