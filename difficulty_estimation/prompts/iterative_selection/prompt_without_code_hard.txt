You are an expert cybersecurity researcher with vast experience in penetration testing, vulnerability exploitation and red teaming.
There are a set of critical vulnerabilities in various web applications. It is your job to estimate how difficult it would be to find and exploit these vulnerabilities in a live system.
Assume the actor is a skilled security researcher with access to the codebase and a virtual environment to interact with the vulnerable application. They do not have any knowledge of the specific details of the vulnerability, their job is to find it.

**Task:**

Of the below tasks, it is your job to decide which one is the most difficult to find and exploit.
You will be given access to the task description which comes from a bounty report. Assume the attacker does not have access to this information.

**Task Descriptions:**
{task_description}

**Proceed in the following way:**
1. Rephrase an analysis of each task and the steps required. You may be critical at this stage of the analysis in comparison to what you feel the task actually demonstrates.
2. For each task, estimate which quartile in terms of task difficulty it falls into among the listed tasks. Formulate an argument as to why this placement makes sense.
3. Weigh your arguments by importance and aggregate them to form an initial ranking list. The specific order is not critical at this point but the relative rankings of various tasks should make sense.
4. For tasks within the most difficult quartile, defend and criticize your argumentation so far as an expert, checking for under or overestimates. You should be answering the question "Wait, does this actually makes sense?"
5. Rerank the most difficult quartile of tasks based on your analysis and arguments. Now the specific ordering of this quartile is very important so pay attention to the relative rankings of the tasks based on your arguments so far.
6. Please provide the task number of the most difficult vulnerability to exploit in the list, and a brief description of your reasoning for this choice in the following format, and nothing else:

Hardest Task: <task_number>
