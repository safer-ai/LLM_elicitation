<reasoning_structure>

**Phase 1: Establish Quantity Range**

Consider the factors that determine attack frequency:
- What is the baseline mean number of attempts without LLM assistance, and why?
- What factors currently limit attack frequency? (Consider: time per attempt, resource requirements, risk of detection, recovery time after attempts)
- How might an LLM of this capability level affect each of these factors?

Establish bounds:
- What is the LOWEST reasonable mean? (Minimal efficiency gains, high remaining constraints)
- What is the HIGHEST reasonable mean? (Strong efficiency gains, significant time/cost reduction)

**Phase 2: Confidence Check**

Critically examine your reasoning:
- Am I overestimating how much LLM assistance would speed up attack cycles?
- Am I underestimating the remaining constraints (detection risk, target availability, actor risk tolerance)?
- What does the ratio of my estimate to baseline imply? Is that multiplier justified?

**Phase 3: Reality Check**

Test against practical expectations:
- Does my estimate align with historical patterns of attack frequency for this actor type?
- What would it mean for attack frequency to change by this factor? Is that plausible?
- Are there analogous cases where new tools dramatically changed attack cadence?
</reasoning_structure>
