<reasoning_structure>
Please follow this structured reasoning process:

**Phase 1: Review and Reassess**

Review the previous round's estimates and rationales:
- What were the key points of agreement across experts?
- What were the main disagreements or different perspectives?
- Were there arguments you hadn't considered that seem valid?
- Were there arguments that seem flawed or unconvincing?

Reassess your probability range in light of this feedback:
- Should your lower bound change? Why or why not?
- Should your upper bound change? Why or why not?

**Phase 2: Confidence Check**

Critically examine whether you should update:
- Am I anchoring too strongly on my previous estimate?
- Am I updating too much based on social pressure rather than valid arguments?
- What is the strongest argument for moving my estimate higher?
- What is the strongest argument for moving my estimate lower?

**Phase 3: Final Assessment**

Synthesise and finalise:
- What is your updated central estimate and why?
- How does your estimate compare to the group distribution from the previous round?
- If you are an outlier, is there good reason for this?
</reasoning_structure>

<distribution_fitting_note>
**Important**: Your estimates will be used to fit a Beta distribution. We elicit three percentiles:
- **25th percentile**: The lower quartile - 25% of the probability mass lies below this value
- **50th percentile**: The median - the midpoint of your distribution
- **75th percentile**: The upper quartile - 75% of the probability mass lies below this value

Together, the 25th and 75th percentiles encompass the inner 50% of your probability mass, providing an uncertainty interval around your median estimate.
</distribution_fitting_note>
